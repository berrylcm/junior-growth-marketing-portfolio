# Junior Growth Marketing Specialist: AI SEO Research Portfolio

## Topic: AI-Powered SEO Content Production

SEO today is not just about keywords and backlinks anymore. AI Overviews and generative search have changed how content gets discovered. AI systems now read correlations across the web, evaluate brand authority, and decide which sources to show in answers, often before a user clicks any link.

Brands that learn how to be recognized, cited, and trusted by AI systems will have a real discoverability advantage. Those that don't will become invisible, even if their content is good.

This research focuses on understanding how AI-powered SEO content production works in practice: what signals matter, how content should be structured, and how brands can build authority that AI systems trust.

---

## Expert Selection

Ten experts were selected across different angles of AI SEO, covering technical fundamentals, automation workflows, and brand strategy.

**Neil Patel** is one of my earliest references when learning digital marketing and SEO. He built NPDigital into one of the largest digital agencies globally. His content now covers GEO and AI search strategy, making him a good anchor for understanding how classic SEO thinking is adapting.

**Nathan Gotch** is an active practitioner who shares real workflows, not just theory. His YouTube channel documents practical AI SEO systems and his work on GEO gives a ground-level view of what is actually working in 2026.

**Marketing Against the Grain** is hosted by HubSpot's CMO and ex-VP Marketing. I was not sure about including a channel tied to a specific tool at first, but the content quality won me over. The conversations bring in solid data and executive-level thinking on AI content strategy.

**Marcus Sheridan** is someone I already knew through his book *Endless Customers*. His core argument is that brands must earn trust by transparently answering buyer questions. This has become even more relevant in AI search, where trust signals directly influence recommendation probability.

**Kevin Indig** is a former SEO lead at Shopify and G2, now a growth advisor. His work focuses on B2B SaaS SEO and GEO strategy, with data-driven takes on AI search behavior and how traditional SEO metrics are shifting.

**Lily Ray** is VP SEO Strategy at Amsive. She is a go-to analyst for Google algorithm updates and E-E-A-T signals. Her research on which content patterns get penalized versus rewarded is directly useful for understanding what AI-era content production should look like.

**Edward Sturm** hosts The Edward Show, a daily SEO podcast with strong guests. His SEO background includes work with Microsoft, P&G, and ADP. He is valuable both for his own tactical content and as an aggregator of current practitioner thinking.

**Nico Gorroño** is the founder of AI Ranking, a community focused on AI-powered SEO workflows and automation. His content is hands-on and implementation-focused, including real workflows built on Claude Code and other AI tools.

**Aleyda Solis** is an international SEO consultant, founder of Orainti, and host of Crawling Mondays. She is one of the most technically detailed voices in the space, with strong coverage of how AI crawlers behave differently from traditional search bots.

**Rand Fishkin** is the founder of Moz and SparkToro. He is a data-driven and often contrarian voice on AI search. His work on zero-click search and influence-based marketing provides a useful counterbalance to more optimistic AI SEO narratives.
